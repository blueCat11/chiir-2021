General information

Later, you will see more information on privacy and your browsing behavior here.

  • What are cookies?

    A cookie is textual information, which can be saved on a user's computer for a visited website. The web server can directly read this cookie information when the user later visits the site again. Purposes of these cookies can be e.g. the identification of the users (i.e. through a session id), saving a login, for example for an online service like Wikipedia, Facebook etc., or saving the contents of a shopping cart with an online vendor.

  • What are third party requests?

    Communication in the internet between a user's computer, and a server works through requests and responses. A third party request is when during the loading of a website, there are not only requests to the server that this website is published on, but also requests to third parties (these are then the third party requests). These third parties can then also receive the information that the current user is visiting this website.

  • What are privacy points?

    This extension awards privacy points for online behavior beneficial to privacy. That may be having a certain browser setting. Privacy points are deducted for online behavior detrimental to privacy, e.g. visiting websites with many cookies and third party requests.
